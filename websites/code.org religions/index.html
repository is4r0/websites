Monotheistic Religions

Monotheism means to only believe in one God. Some Monotheistic Religions are Islam, hristianity, Judaism, and Zoroastrianism.Judaism, Christianity, and Islam are called the Abrahamic religions because they all trace back to the Prophet Abraham. Judaism is the oldest one, teaching belief in one God (Yahweh) and following the Torah. Christianity evolved from Judaism, teaching that Jesus is the Son of God and Savior, with the Bible as its holy book. Islam was founded by Prophet Muhammad, teaches strict belief in one God (Allah), and follows the Qurâ€™an. Zoroastrianism is not Abrahamic because it was created before Prophet Abraham was born, but it is one of the oldest religions in the world. Zoroastrianism was founded in Persia by Zoroaster, teaching the struggle between good (Ahura Mazda) and evil (Angra Mainyu). Their holy book is the Avesta.

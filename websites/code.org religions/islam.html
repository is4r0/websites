Islam


Islam is the newest of the 3 Abrahamic religions. It was founded in the 7th century by Prophet Muhammad . It teaches strict belief in one God, called Allah, and follows the holy book called the Qur’an. Muslims believe that Prophet Muhammad was the final messenger, completing the messages of earlier prophets like Moses and Jesus. Islam teaches that humans should worship God alone and follow his guidance. The main principles are called the Five Pillars: Shahada (declaration of faith), Salah (prayer), Zakat (charity), Sawm (fasting in Ramadan), and Hajj (pilgrimage to Makkah). Muslims pray five times a day facing the Kaaba in Makkah. Imams lead the main prayer in mosques. Muslims believe in the Day of Judgment, when everyone will be held accountable for their deeds. There are two main branches: Sunni and Shia, with different practices but the same belief in Allah and the Qur’an. Islam also shares many prophets and stories with Judaism and Christianity since they have similar prophets as Islam. Major holidays include Eid al-Fitr after Ramadan and Eid al-Adha during Hajj. Islam spread quickly across the world and today has over 2 billion followers. To summarize, Islam focuses on worshiping Allah, following god's guidance, and preparing for the afterlife.

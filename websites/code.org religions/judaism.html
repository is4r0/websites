Judaism


Judaism is one of the oldest monotheistic religions, tracing its roots back to Prophet Abraham. It teaches belief in one God, known as Yahweh, and follows the sacred text called the Torah. The Torah contains the laws and teachings that guide Jewish life and practices. Judaism places strong importance on following God’s commandments and living a moral life. One of the most well-known practices is keeping the Sabbath, a day of rest and worship. Jewish people worship in synagogues, led by religious leaders called rabbis. They also observe many festivals, such as Passover, which remembers the Exodus from Egypt. Judaism emphasizes the idea of a covenant, or special promise, between God and the Jewish people. This covenant means they are chosen to follow God’s laws. There are different branches of Judaism, including Orthodox, Conservative, and Reform. Each group interprets the teachings of the Torah differently. Judaism also teaches hope for the coming of the Messiah, a future leader who will bring peace. It has deeply influenced both Christianity and Islam, making it very important in world history. Overall, Judaism centers on worshiping one God, following his laws, and living a righteous life.
